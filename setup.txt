OW Scout - quick setup
=======================

1. Put owscout-testing.exe in its own folder (e.g. C:\OWScout\) and run it.
   - Windows may show a blue "Windows protected your PC" box. This is normal for
     a new app that isn't code-signed yet - it is NOT a virus warning.
     Click:  More info  ->  Run anyway.

2. IMPORTANT - set Overwatch to BORDERLESS or FULLSCREEN at your monitor's
   native resolution (Options -> Video -> Display Mode). Do NOT run it in a
   window - the calibration boxes will miss the portraits if you do.
   Then open any replay so the row of 10 hero portraits shows along the top.

3. In OW Scout, the app opens on the SETTINGS tab. Click "Calibrate to my
   screen". It draws the boxes for you - if the green boxes sit ON the portraits,
   press ENTER to save. If they're clearly off, press ESC and drag the two boxes
   by hand (one around each team's 5 portraits, ENTER after each). Keep Overwatch
   in the SAME display mode for calibrating AND capturing.

4. It switches you to the SCOUT tab. Click "Sync codes from FACEIT" once. The
   first time it downloads the match list (a few seconds).

5. Scout a game:
   - Pick an Opponent and a Replay from the dropdowns, then copy the code into
     Overwatch's replay screen.
   - Click "Start hotkey capture", then watch the replay and press:
        F8 = snapshot the current comp (press it whenever heroes change)
        F7 = next round      F9 = undo      F10 = done (finish capturing)
     (Do NOT use Esc - that opens the Overwatch menu. You can also click these
      buttons on the on-screen overlay. Drag the overlay by its title bar if it's
      in the way.)

6. When you've captured a game, click "Review captured maps" to check it, then
   "Publish my captures". That's it - your scouting appears on the site in a
   couple of minutes.

The banner at the top always tells you the next step. Anything unclear - ask.
